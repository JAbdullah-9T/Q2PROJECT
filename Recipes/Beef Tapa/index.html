Beef Tapa


Ingredients (3-4 Serves)

  • 1 lb (500 g) beef sirloin or tenderloin, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 3 tbsp calamansi juice (or lemon juice as a substitute)
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil (for frying)

Instructions

  • Slice the beef thinly against the grain. If the beef is slightly frozen, itâ€™s easier to cut thin slices.
  • In a bowl, combine soy sauce, calamansi juice, sugar, garlic, and black pepper.
  • Add the beef slices and mix well, ensuring all pieces are coated.
  • Cover and marinate in the fridge for at least 2 hours or overnight for better flavor.
  • Heat cooking oil in a frying pan over medium heat.
  • Fry the beef slices in batches, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Cook for about 2-3 minutes on each side or until browned and cooked through.
  • Remove from the pan and set aside.
  • Serve the beef tapa hot with garlic fried rice (sinangag), sunny-side-up egg, and a side of atchara (pickled papaya) or fresh tomatoes. Enjoy!
