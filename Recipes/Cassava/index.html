Cassava


Ingredients (Makes 20-25 rolls)

    For the Cassava Mixture:
  • 2 lbs (about 900g) grated cassava (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar (optional, adjust sweetness as needed)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
    • For the Topping:
  • 1/2 can (7 oz) coconut milk (or use fresh coconut cream)
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1/4 tsp salt

Instructions

  • If using fresh cassava, peel the cassava root and then grate it using a fine grater. If using frozen grated cassava, thaw it completely and drain any excess water.
  • In a large mixing bowl, combine the grated cassava, sweetened condensed milk, coconut milk, sugar, eggs, melted butter, salt, and vanilla extract. Mix well until everything is fully combined and smooth.
  • Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  • Grease a 9x9-inch baking dish or a similar-sized pan with butter or cooking spray.
  • Pour the cassava mixture into the prepared baking dish, spreading it evenly.
  • Bake the cassava cake in the preheated oven for 45-60 minutes or until the top is golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  • While the cassava cake is baking, prepare the topping. In a saucepan, combine the coconut milk, sugar, cornstarch, and salt.
  • Stir the mixture over medium heat until it thickens and becomes smooth (this usually takes about 3-5 minutes). Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.
  • Once the cassava cake is finished baking, remove it from the oven and pour the coconut topping evenly over the hot cake.
  • Return the cassava cake to the oven and bake for another 10-15 minutes or until the topping is set and slightly golden.
  • Let the cassava cake cool to room temperature before slicing into squares.
  • For an extra indulgent touch, you can chill the cake in the refrigerator for a few hours before serving. The flavors will develop further, and the texture will be even more delicious! Enjoy!
