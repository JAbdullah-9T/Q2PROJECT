Kare-Kare


Ingredients (6-8 Serves)

    For the Meat and Broth:
  • 2 lbs (1 kg) oxtail
  • 8 cups water
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 4 cloves garlic, crushed
  • Salt and pepper to taste
    • For the Kare-Kare Sauce:
  • 1 cup unsweetened peanut butter (or ground roasted peanuts)
  • 2 tbsp toasted rice flour (toast glutinous rice and grind into powder)
  • 2 tbsp annatto powder (for color; substitute with annatto seeds soaked in water)
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
    • Vegetables:
  • 1 bunch bok choy (pechay)
  • 1 cup eggplant, sliced diagonally
  • 1 cup string beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 banana heart (puso ng saging), sliced (optional)

Instructions

  • Boil the oxtail (or your chosen meat) in water with onions, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  • Simmer until tender (about 2-3 hours, or 35-45 minutes in a pressure cooker). Skim off scum and impurities.
  • Once cooked, remove the meat and set aside. Reserve the broth for the sauce.
  • Blanch the vegetables (bok choy, eggplant, string beans, banana heart) in boiling water until slightly tender. Set aside.
  • In a pot, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the annatto powder (or annatto water) to give the sauce its distinct orange color.
  • Pour in the reserved broth (about 4-5 cups).
  • Add the peanut butter and stir until well blended.
  • Slowly sprinkle and whisk in the toasted rice flour to thicken the sauce. Let it simmer for about 5-7 minutes until the sauce is creamy.
  • Add the cooked meat into the sauce and let it simmer for 10 minutes to absorb the flavors.
  • Add the blanched vegetables and cook for another 3-5 minutes. Be careful not to overcook.
  • Serve the Kare-Kare hot in a large bowl with rice. Enjoy!
