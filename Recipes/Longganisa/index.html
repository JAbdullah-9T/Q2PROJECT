Longganisa


Ingredients (Makes 12-15 sausages)

    For the Sausage:
  • 1 lb (500 g) ground beef or ground chicken
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp vinegar (white or apple cider)
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp paprika (optional, for color)
  • 1/2 tsp salt
    • For the Wrapping (Optional:
  • Sausage casings (Optional, for traditional presentation)
  • Parchment paper or plastic wrap (for skinless version)
    • For Cooking:
  • Cooking oil, for frying
  • Water, for steaming

Instructions

  • In a large bowl, combine the ground meat, minced garlic, brown sugar, soy sauce, vinegar, black pepper, paprika, salt, and ground cloves. Mix well until fully incorporated.
  • Cover the mixture with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour (or overnight) to allow the flavors to meld.
  • Take about 2-3 tablespoons of the mixture and shape it into a log (about 3-4 inches long).
  • Wrap each log in parchment paper or plastic wrap and twist the ends to seal.
  • Optional: Stuff the meat mixture into halal-certified sausage casings using a funnel or sausage stuffer. Twist into individual links.
  • In a skillet, add about 1/4 cup water and place the longganisa in a single layer.
  • Cover and cook over medium heat until the water evaporates and the sausages release their natural oils.
  • Add a small amount of cooking oil and fry the sausages until golden brown and caramelized.
  • Serve the longganisa hot with steamed rice, a fried egg, and a side of vinegar dipping sauce or tomato ketchup. Enjoy!
