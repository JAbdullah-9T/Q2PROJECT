Sinigang


Ingredients (4-6 Serves)

    For the Protein:
  • 1.5 lbs (700 g) beef (you can substitute with shrimp, or fish)
  • 6 cups water
    • For the Sour Broth:
  • 1 packet tamarind soup mix (sinigang mix) or 1 cup fresh tamarind pulp
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 2 medium tomatoes, quartered
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce (patis)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Vegetables:
  • 1 cup radish (labanos), sliced into thin rounds
  • 1 cup eggplant, sliced diagonally
  • 1 cup string beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 bunch water spinach (kangkong) or bok choy
  • 2 green finger chilies (siling pangsigang) (optional, for mild spice)

Instructions

  • Boil the pork in water in a large pot over medium heat. Skim off the scum as it rises.
  • Once the broth is clear, add the onion and tomatoes. Simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until the pork is tender. (For beef, simmer longer, and for shrimp, add later in the process.
  • Stir in the tamarind mix or fresh tamarind pulp. If using fresh tamarind: (Boil tamarind pods in water, mash the pulp, and strain the juice into the soup.)
  • Add fish sauce for depth of flavor.
  • Add the radish and cook for 5 minutes.
  • Add the eggplant, string beans, okra (if using), and chilies. Cook for another 5-7 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  • Add the water spinach (kangkong) or bok choy last, as they cook quickly.
  • Adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, or additional fish sauce to taste.
  • Serve hot in a large bowl with steamed rice on the side. Enjoy!
