Tortang Talong


Ingredients (2-3 Serves)

  • 3 medium-sized eggplants (talong)
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
  • Cooking oil, for frying

Instructions

  • Wash the eggplants thoroughly and pat them dry.
  • Roast the eggplants over an open flame, grill, or broil in the oven until the skin is charred and wrinkled. This makes it easier to peel and adds a smoky flavor.
  • Once cooled, peel off the charred skin, leaving the stem intact.
  • Gently flatten the eggplants with a fork, creating a fan-like shape.
  • In a bowl, beat the eggs with salt and pepper.
  • If using add-ins like meat or vegetables, mix them into the beaten eggs.
  • Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and add a small amount of cooking oil.
  • Dip the flattened eggplant into the egg mixture, ensuring it is well-coated.
  • Place the coated eggplant onto the hot pan. Pour some of the remaining egg mixture on top to cover the eggplant further.
  • Cook for 3-4 minutes per side, or until the egg is golden brown and set. Flip carefully using a spatula.
  • Transfer the cooked Tortang Talong to a plate.
  • Serve hot with steamed rice and banana ketchup or a soy sauce-vinegar dipping sauce. Enjoy!
