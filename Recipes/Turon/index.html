Turon


Ingredients (Makes 6-8 rolls)

  • 3-4 ripe saba bananas (or plantains, peeled and sliced into strips)
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar (or white sugar)
  • 1/2 cup jackfruit (optional, cut into strips; fresh or canned)
  • Cooking oil, for frying
  • Cinnamon powder (optional, for extra flavor)/li>
  • Spring roll wrappers (around 6-8, depending on how much filling you use per piece)

Instructions

  • Peel and slice the saba bananas: Cut the bananas into strips (around 2-3 pieces per banana depending on size).
  • Prepare the jackfruit: If you are using fresh or canned jackfruit, cut it into strips. This is optional, but it adds a nice flavor and texture to the turon.
  • In a shallow bowl, place the brown sugar. Optionally, add a sprinkle of cinnamon powder to the sugar for extra flavor.
  • Roll each banana slice in the sugar until it’s evenly coated. If you’re using jackfruit, coat the strips in the sugar as well.
  • Lay a spring roll wrapper flat on a clean surface, with one corner facing you (like a diamond shape).
  • Place a coated banana strip on the wrapper, then add a few pieces of jackfruit beside it (optional).
  • Fold the bottom corner over the filling, then fold the sides inward. Roll tightly, securing the filling inside. Wet the top edge with a little water to seal the wrapper.
  • Heat a generous amount of cooking oil in a pan over medium heat. The oil should be enough to submerge the turon halfway.
  • Once the oil is hot, fry the wrapped turon in batches, turning occasionally, until the wrappers are golden brown and crispy (about 3-5 minutes).
  • Once fried, remove the turon from the oil and place it on a paper towel to drain excess oil.
  • Let the turon cool slightly before serving. It’s delicious on its own or with a dusting of extra sugar.
  • Serve warm for a crispy, sweet, and gooey treat! Enjoy!
